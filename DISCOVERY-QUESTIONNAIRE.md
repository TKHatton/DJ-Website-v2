# Agent Build Discovery Questionnaire
*Walk through with client on discovery call. Let your notetaker capture everything.*

---

## 1. The Problem (5 min)

**Start here. Let them talk.**

- "Walk me through the workflow or process you want to improve."
- "Where does it slow down or break?"
- "What triggers this process? How often does it happen?"
- "What happens when something goes wrong today?"

**Listen for:** Manual steps, waiting, copy-paste, context switching, forgotten tasks

---

## 2. Current Tools & Data (5 min)

**Understand their ecosystem.**

- "What tools do you currently use for this?" (CRM, spreadsheets, Slack, email, etc.)
- "Where does the data live? Is it in one place or scattered?"
- "Are there any tools you wish talked to each other?"
- "Do you have APIs or integrations already set up?"

**Listen for:** Tool sprawl, manual data entry, disconnected systems

---

## 3. The Human Judgment Question (5 min)
**This defines what agents can vs. cannot do.**

- "At what points in this process do you need a human to make a decision?"
- "What decisions are routine vs. require real expertise?"
- "Are there edge cases that always need a human?"
- "What would you never want automated?"

**Listen for:** Approval workflows, exceptions, client-facing decisions, compliance

---

## 4. Who Uses This (3 min)

- "Who's involved in this process today?"
- "Who would interact with the new system?"
- "What's their comfort level with new tools?"
- "Do they need to see inside the system or just get outputs?"

---

## 5. Success Metrics (5 min)

**How will they know it worked?**

- "If we built this perfectly, what changes?"
- "How much time are you spending on this now?"
- "What would success look like in 30 days? 90 days?"
- "Is there a number you're trying to hit?" (time saved, leads processed, etc.)

---

## 6. Constraints & Concerns (5 min)

**Surface blockers early.**

- "Are there any compliance or security requirements I should know about?"
- "Who needs to approve changes or new tools?"
- "What's been tried before? What didn't work?"
- "What concerns do you have about automation?"

---

## 7. Timeline & Priority (3 min)

- "When do you need this working by?"
- "Is there an event or deadline driving this?"
- "If we could only solve one piece of this, what matters most?"

---

## 8. Budget Reality Check (2 min)

*Frame as partnership, not sales.*

- "Have you set aside budget for this, or are we exploring what's possible?"
- "Is this a one-time build or ongoing relationship?"
- "Who else needs to sign off on investment?"

---

## Closing the Call

1. **Summarize back:** "Here's what I heard..." (2-3 sentences)
2. **Identify the core problem:** "The main thing we'd solve is..."
3. **Set expectations:** "I'll put together some thoughts on how we might approach this."
4. **Next step:** "I'll send over a brief summary and we can schedule a follow-up to discuss the approach."

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- No clear owner or decision maker
- "We need this yesterday" + no budget conversation
- Scope that keeps expanding during the call
- They want code/repos, not outcomes
- Compliance requirements they're vague about

## Green Flags

- Clear pain point with measurable impact
- Decision maker on the call
- Realistic timeline
- Already using some automation (shows readiness)
- Asking good questions about how it would work

---

## After the Call

1. Review notetaker transcript
2. Draft 1-page summary: Problem, Proposed approach, Rough scope, Next steps
3. Send within 24 hours
4. Schedule follow-up within the week
