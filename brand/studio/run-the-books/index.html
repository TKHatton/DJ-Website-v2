What it does

The financial spine for a one-person business.

Most solo operators run their finances in a stack of mismatched tools. A spreadsheet for tracking. A free invoicing app that won't let you customize anything. A separate expense tracker. A folder of receipts. A monthly panic at tax time.

Run the Books replaces the stack. One desktop app. Your data on your machine. Invoicing that looks like your brand. Expense tracking that doesn't make you log in to anything. Money flow you can see at a glance. No subscription, no cloud, no surprises.

"Built to run quietly in the background while you do the work."

02 / What's included

The full operating layer.

  • Branded invoicing with PDF export
  • Expense tracking with category rules
  • Money-in and money-out cash flow view
  • Recurring invoice and payment templates
  • Batch billing for repeat clients
  • Client health scoring (paying late, falling off, growing)
  • Forecasting based on real history, not vibes
  • Tax export at year end. Clean handoff to your accountant.
  • Proposal templates that convert into invoices
  • Local SQLite database. Your data stays on your machine.
  • One-time license. No subscription.
  • Lifetime updates included.

03 / How it works

Four steps. The app does the rest.

  1. Set up your business. Brand, services, tax setup, default rates. Once. Stays.
  2. Bill clients. Send branded invoices in a few clicks. Track who's paid, who's late, who's been quiet.
  3. Log expenses. Categorize once. Run the Books learns your patterns and tags new ones for you.
  4. See the picture. Cash flow, forecast, client health, year-to-date. One screen, every Monday morning.
